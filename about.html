About Me

My path into project management runs straight through engineering. I studied Computer Science & Engineering, which taught me to think in systems, dependencies, and trade-offs — then I discovered I was even more energized by the part that sits around the build: aligning people, sequencing the work, surfacing risk early, and getting things over the line. So I earned my MS in Project Management at Northeastern and my Professional Scrum Master certification, and I've spent the last 3+ years delivering projects across management services, healthcare/clinical compliance, and technology.

I've directed an end-to-end 150-person compliance project to on-time delivery with zero scope gaps, run 4 concurrent implementations with zero schedule overruns, and repeatedly used process analysis to cut documentation and reporting effort by 30–38%. What ties it together: I plan deliberately, communicate clearly, and remove blockers before they become fires.

How I work

  • I adapt the method to the project — Agile/Scrum for fast, iterative product work; Waterfall for structured, high-governance delivery; and hybrids when reality calls for it.
  • I build the artifacts that keep projects honest: project & communication plans, test plans, risk/issue logs, process flows, and executive status reporting.
  • I'm hands-on with MS Project, Smartsheet, SharePoint, Jira, Asana, Confluence, Power BI, and the full Microsoft Office suite.
  • I measure success by on-time, on-budget delivery, clean scope, and stakeholders who are never surprised.

My Computer Science background means I'm comfortable partnering closely with engineering teams and reasoning about technical trade-offs — which is why I'm focused on project and product management roles in technology.
