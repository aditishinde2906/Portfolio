Project Portfolio Reporting Dashboard

Portfolio-level reporting automation across 6 concurrent workstreams

Role: Project Manager / Reporting Lead  ·  Scope: 6 concurrent workstreams  ·  Tools: Power BI, Tableau, Smartsheet, Excel

The Challenge

Running six concurrent workstreams meant six separate trackers, six status formats, and a recurring scramble to hand-assemble a portfolio view before every leadership update. The compilation was slow, error-prone, and always slightly out of date by the time it was read — which meant delivery risk and slipping milestones surfaced later than they should. Leadership needed a single, trustworthy, real-time picture of the whole portfolio, and the team needed those hours back.

My Approach

  • Standardized the source data. Aligned the six workstream trackers in Smartsheet on a common schema — consistent status fields, milestone dates, RAG indicators, and budget figures — so the data could roll up cleanly instead of being reconciled by hand each cycle.
  • Connected live, not copied. Linked Power BI and Tableau directly to the live Smartsheet trackers, so the dashboards reflected the current state of work rather than a snapshot pasted into a deck. No more manual exports.
  • Designed for the leadership question. Built the views around what leadership actually needed to decide on: portfolio-wide milestone status, delivery-risk heat across workstreams, and budget/schedule health — surfacing the exceptions that needed attention rather than burying them in detail.
  • Automated the refresh. Set up scheduled refreshes so the portfolio view stayed current on its own, turning a manual weekly compilation into a self-updating, always-on report.
  • Two tools, by audience. Used Power BI and Tableau to serve different consumers and reporting habits, keeping a consistent data story across both.

Challenges & How I Solved Them

  • Inconsistent source trackers: the six workstreams had drifted into different formats. Standardizing the Smartsheet schema first was the unglamorous step that made everything downstream possible — automation can't fix data that doesn't line up.
  • Stale, manual reporting: replacing copy-paste compilation with a live connection removed both the lag and the transcription errors, so the number leadership saw was the number in the tracker.
  • Signal over noise: a portfolio dashboard can easily become an unreadable wall of metrics. I designed the views around exceptions and risk first, so the things that needed a decision stood out at a glance.

Results & Deliverables

  • Reduced reporting cycle time by 35% by replacing manual compilation with live-linked, auto-refreshing dashboards.
  • Freed 4+ hours per week previously spent assembling portfolio status by hand.
  • Gave leadership real-time visibility into delivery risk and milestone status across all 6 workstreams.
  • Delivered automated Power BI and Tableau dashboards connected to live Smartsheet trackers, plus a standardized tracker schema that keeps the data clean at the source.

What I Took Away

The biggest lesson was that reporting problems are usually data-structure problems in disguise. Once the six trackers shared a schema, the dashboards almost built themselves — and the real payoff wasn't the visuals, it was giving leadership a current, trustworthy view and giving the team its hours back. I now treat "make the source consistent" as the first step in any reporting work, not an afterthought.
